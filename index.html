About Me

I am a highly motivated Cyber Security Analyst with a strong foundation in information security, threat detection, and incident response. I have hands-on experience working in simulated SOC environments, where I actively monitored security events, analyzed logs, and investigated potential cyber threats to protect organizational assets. My expertise includes security monitoring, SIEM-based log analysis, intrusion detection, vulnerability assessment, and risk analysis. I have worked extensively with tools such as Splunk, Suricata, Zeek, Sysinternals, and various OSINT platforms to identify Indicators of Compromise (IOCs), analyze attacker behavior, and respond to security incidents efficiently. Through real-world cybersecurity projects, I have investigated compromised systems, analyzed malware behavior, and conducted in-depth incident response activities. I am experienced in mapping attacker techniques to the MITRE ATT&CK framework, preparing structured incident reports, and recommending mitigation strategies to strengthen security posture and prevent future attacks. I possess strong knowledge of networking fundamentals, including TCP/IP, DNS, HTTP/HTTPS, and system-level security across Linux and Windows environments. My programming background in Python, Bash, and Java supports automation, log analysis, and security workflow optimization. I am highly detail-oriented, analytical, and passionate about cybersecurity. I continuously strive to improve my skills by participating in hands-on labs, simulations, and certifications such as Google Cybersecurity Professional Certificate and TryHackMe SOC Level 1. My goal is to contribute to a security-focused organization by detecting threats early, strengthening defenses, and helping build resilient systems.
